The Best Movies According to Ritvika

My top three movies

Home Alone

"Home Alone" is a beloved Christmas classic released in 1990, directed by Chris Columbus and written by John Hughes. The film stars Macaulay Culkin as Kevin McCallister, an eight-year-old boy who is accidentally left behind when his family goes on a Christmas vacation to Paris.

"Home Alone" shines with its blend of funny slapstick comedy and touching moments. The film captures the joy and freedom of childhood while showcasing Kevin's cleverness and bravery. As he cleverly outsmarts two goofy burglars, we see his resourcefulness and courage, which feels both inspiring and amusing. The movie beautifully explores themes of family, growing up, and the special magic of Christmas. Its heartfelt story and clever humor make it relatable and delightful, creating a perfect mix of laughs and warmth that resonates with audiences of all ages.

A Beautiful Mind

"A Beautiful Mind" (2001) is a critically acclaimed film directed by Ron Howard and starring Russell Crowe. It is based on the life of John Nash, a brilliant mathematician who struggled with schizophrenia.

The film chronicles Nash's rise from a promising student at Princeton to a Nobel Prize-winning mathematician. It delves deeply into his personal and professional life, focusing on his groundbreaking work in game theory. Nash's achievements are overshadowed by his battle with schizophrenia, which leads him to experience hallucinations and delusions. Despite these challenges, he finds support from his wife, Alicia (played by Jennifer Connelly), and eventually overcomes many of his obstacles to achieve professional success.

Pride & Prejudice

"Pride & Prejudice" is a celebrated film adaptation of Jane Austen's classic novel, first released in 2005 and directed by Joe Wright. This film iscelebrated for its sharp social commentary, intricate characters, and romantic narrative. Set in early 19th-century England, it provides a witty exploration of class, marriage, and morality.

The story revolves around Elizabeth Bennet, one of five sisters in the Bennet family, and her evolving relationship with the wealthy and aloof Mr. Darcy. The novel begins with the Bennet family's concern over marrying off their daughters, particularly Elizabeth, who is intelligent, independent, and often at odds with societal expectations. Mr. Darcy, initially perceived as arrogant and proud, gradually reveals his true character through his actions and interactions with Elizabeth. As misunderstandings and social prejudices create tension between Elizabeth and Mr. Darcy, both characters undergo significant personal growth. Elizabeth learns to look beyond first impressions and prejudices, while Mr. Darcy confronts his own flaws and societal biases. Their journey toward mutual respect and love ultimately challenges the social norms of their time.
